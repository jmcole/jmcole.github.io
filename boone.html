Daniel Boone Tribute Page

The Original Pioneer

Daniel Boone leads settlers across the Cumberland Gap

Here's a time line of Daniel's Life:

  • 1734 -Born in Berks County, Pennsylvania
  • 1750 -Family moves to Yadkin Valley, North Carolina
  • 1755 -Serves with British Army during French and Indian War
  • 1755 -Meets John Finley who tells him of the wonders of Kentucky
  • 1756 -Marries Rebecca Bryan
  • 1758 -Serves in North Carolina militia during Cherokee Uprising
  • 1765 -Boone's father dies
  • 1767 -Boone's first longhunt in Kentucky
  • 1769 -Begins two year hunt of Kentucky
  • 1773 -Family settles in Kentucky
  • 1773 -Oldest son is killed by Indians
  • 1774 -Serves in militia during Dunmore's War
  • 1775 -Build's Wilderness Road
  • 1775 -Creates fortified settlement at Boonesborough
  • 1776 -Revolutionary War begins
  • 1776 -Jemima (daughter) captured by Indians and rescued by Daniel.
  • 1777 -Boonesborough attacked by British/Indians
  • 1778 -Boone and salt boiling party captured by Shawnee. Boone escapes.
  • 1778 -Boonesborough repulses siege by British/Indian force
  • 1780 -Boone joins George Rogers Clark invasion of Ohio
  • 1782 -Boone fights in last Battle of Revolutionary War. Loses son at Battle of Blue Licks
  • 1799 -Leaves the United States and settles in Spanish Louisiana in Missouri
  • 1820 -Died in Femme Osage, Missouri

"Many heroic actions and chivalrous adventures are related of me which exist only in the regions of fancy. With me the world has taken great liberties, and yet I have been but a common man."

Daniel Boone
