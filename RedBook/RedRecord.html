A Red Record

In slave times the Negro was kept subservient and submissive by the frequency and severity of the scourging, but, with freedom, a new system of intimidation came into vogue; the Negro was not only whipped and scourged; he was killed.

Lynchings at the Curve

In Memphis, TN a group of three black businessmen, Thomas Moss, Calvin McDowell, and Henry Stewart, operated the People’s Grocery Company. In March 1892, a mob of white men, possibly incited by a owner of a competing grocery, entered People’s and attacked the owners. While defending themselves and their place of business, several white men were injured during an exchange of gunfire. The three men were arrested and placed in jail. Several days later a mob attacked the jail and drug the three men out where they were shot and killed. Thomas Collins was a friend of Ida. B. Wells.

Campaign to Stop Lynching

After the death of her friend, Ida used her position as editor and co-owner of the newspaper The Free Speech and Headlight to speak out against the crime. This action resulted in a white mob descending upon her office and destroying it and threatening her to never return to the city of Memphis again. However, Ms. Wells did not back down. Her response would be to scientifically analyze these incidents and revolutionize the field of journalism. It may be hard to believe today, but even white progressives believed that lynching was justified. Ms. Wells quantified the reported incidents she found in newspapers and built an argument that most lynchings were the result of racial hatred and envy rather than a response to real crimes. In fact, many of these events were pre-planned.

A Red Record

In 1895, Ms. Wells published A Red Record. This 100 page pamphlet went into detail on cases of lynching across the US. With 14 pages of statistics, she provided ample evidence of the extent of the use of lynching across the US. This was particularly suprising to many in the North, as her data shows the majority of lynching occured in the South. However, lynching did not just occur in the South.
